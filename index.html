About me

I am Xinyue Lan, a senior studying Communications Engineering at the joint school of UoG and UESTC.

My research interests are relevant to robotics, especially about visual SLAM, visual odometry and path planning for UGV and UAV. I have been working on Ant Colony Optimization in path planning and Zero Velocity Update (ZUPT) based on inertial navigation for tracking. Currently, my work is related to the participation of deep learning in optimization of visual odometry in order to improve the robustness of visual SLAM in highly dynamic scenes such as shopping malls and corridors.

Besides, I am a huge fan of movies and detective fictions, especially Agatha Christie and David Fincher (I’m crazy about them!)

Projects

Curvilinear path generation for UGV with improved ant colony algorithm

We proposed an efficient approach through combining improved Ant Colony Optimization (ACO) algorithm with cubic spline parametrization to provide smooth global route. After the grid map is constructed, initial cost policy is introduced for ant to search, and performance of ant colony algorithm is improved through adding A* heuristic search and MAX_MIN ant system characteristics to enhance global searching ability. An evaluation function based on novel cost policy is also introduced to evaluate path candidates in order to improve the quality of global path.

Adaptive Zero Velocity Update in Inertial Tracking Based on Neural Network

Zero Velocity Update (ZUPT) has played a key role in Pedestrian Dead Reckoning (PDR) with inertial measurement units (IMU). The accumulation of a large number of points in inertial navigation will produce inevitable error which leads to track detection problems. A model based on deep neural networks was proposed to determine the moments when the ZUPT should be conducted adaptively, which ensures robust performance regardless of different motion types.

Visual SLAM with Deep Learning in Dynamic Scenes

In order to improve the robustness of classic visual SLAM in highly dynamic scenes such as shopping malls and corridors, we embedded YOLO, a deep learning networks in ORB-SLAM2 for dynamic object detection and filtering. The system outperformed the state-of-the-art visual SLAM system by 80% in tracking robustness in dynamic scenes.

Rover Design in Webots to Complete Specific Tasks in Virtual Patios

A low-cost vehicle equipped with multiple sensors is designed in Webots to complete specific tasks including color recognition, path following and dropping virtual fish food into pond.
